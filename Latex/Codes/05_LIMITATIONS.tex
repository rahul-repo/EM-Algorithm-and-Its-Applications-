\section{LIMITATIONS}
While the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm offers notable advantages in segmenting brain tumors from MRI images, it also presents certain limitations:

1.We assume that the underlying probability distributions of image intensities follow a Gaussian distribution(Observing the histogram of intensity and number of pixels). However, this assumption may not always hold true for MRI images, especially in the presence of complex tissue structures, artifacts, or heterogeneous tumor characteristics.

2. MRI images may contain noise and artifacts that can adversely affect the performance of the EM algorithm.Due to the absence of modern software, we are unable to effectively reduce noise.

3.These algorithm require a significant amount of time to differentiate between tumor and non-tumor region.

4.The performance of the EM algorithm may vary across different datasets and tumor types.
